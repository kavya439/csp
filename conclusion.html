INCIDENCE OF DIABETES AND CHRONIC DISEASES

CONCLUSION

Based on the survey findings, it is evident that while there is a general awareness of diabetes and chronic diseases, there is still room for improvement in public understanding and access to healthcare. The recommendations outlined in the report, such as increased public education, improved healthcare access, and community-based programs, can help address these gaps and improve the overall health of the population.

Specifically, the survey revealed:
Knowledge gaps: While many respondents were aware of diabetes, there was a lack of understanding regarding specific risk factors, prevention strategies, 
and treatment options.
Healthcare barriers: Cost, lack of insurance, and limited availability of healthcare services were identified as significant obstacles to accessing care.
Community needs: There is a demand for more community-based programs that provide support, education, and resources for individuals with 
chronic diseases.

By addressing these issues through targeted interventions, we can work towards reducing the 
prevalence of chronic diseases,improving quality of life for affected individuals, and ultimately building a healthier society.
